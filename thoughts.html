    The Death and Life of Great American Cities

    I read the book The Death and Life of Great American Cities by Jane Jacobs this summer. It is a groundbreaking work that challenges conventional urban planning principles and celebrates the complexity of city life.

    Jacobs critiques the top-down planning approaches that prioritize large-scale development and segregation of uses, advocating instead for the organic, diverse, and mixed-use neighborhoods that foster vibrant communities.

    Her emphasis on the importance of street life has reshaped how we understand what makes cities livable and safe.
