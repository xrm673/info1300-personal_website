    Healthy Streets for Beijing

    Working with the Beijing Municipal Institute of City Planning and Design (BMICPD), I conducted an on-site survey to analyze the current conditions of Chaowai Street and evaluated the street using the Healthy Street framework from London, identifying 11 key areas for improvement.

    Leveraging tools like AutoCAD and SketchUp, I developed a revised street model that was ultimately adopted in the street's reconstruction, contributing to the enhancement of urban design and public health in the area.

    Spatial Analysis for Atlanta

    In my project, Concrete Barriers: How Atlanta's Expressway Shaped Division, I conducted a GIS analysis to examine the impact of expressways on Atlanta's urban landscape and local communities.

    By utilizing census data, I contrasted the socioeconomic and demographic characteristics—such as income, race, and business presence—of neighborhoods on either side of the expressways.

    This analysis highlighted how these infrastructures contribute to segregation and economic disparities, revealing the profound role expressways play in shaping the city's development and social fabric.
